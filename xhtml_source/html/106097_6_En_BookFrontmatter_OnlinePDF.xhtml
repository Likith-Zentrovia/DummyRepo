Preface

I’m delighted to invite you to read and learn from this new 6th edition of Diagnostic Interviewing. Having a 6th edition of a book suggests that its value has withstood the test of time, that it continues to serve a useful purpose, namely to help emerging and seasoned clinicians interview, assess, and understand their clients more effectively, and for that I’m very grateful. Of course, this new edition did not occur in a vacuum. Indeed, I owe a substantial debt of gratitude to the prior editors, Michel Hersen and Samuel M. Turner, who co-led the first three editions of this book. After Sam’s untimely death, Michel invited me to join the team and we co-edited the 4th edition together, which was published in 2010. After Michel’s retirement in 2011, I served as the sole editor for the 5th edition (published in 2019), a role I continue to serve for this new edition. Michel was a great mentor and friend to me since we first met in 1992, and I remain greatly appreciative of all that he has taught me about writing, publishing, supervising, mentoring, and teaching.

The vision of this book has not changed since its inception many years ago. Indeed, one of the most difficult milestones in a new clinician’s career is the completion of the first interview with a real live client (as opposed to role-playing with volunteers or peers). Such endeavor is generally fraught with significant apprehension. However, if the interview goes well, there is much rejoicing. However, if the interview falls flat, there will be considerable consternation on the part of the clinician and usually heavy doses of constructive feedback from the supervisor. Regardless of the amount of preparation that has taken place before the interview, emerging clinicians will justifiably remain apprehensive about this endeavor. Thus, this new edition of Diagnostic Interviewing, like the prior editions, remains devoted to providing clear guidance and structure for new and seasoned clinicians in successfully engaging with a large variety of clients and presenting problems in diverse interviewing settings. I sincerely hope that readers of this book find many “pearls of wisdom” in these pages, and that this book helps them become more prepared, confident, and effective clinicians.

In consideration of the positive response to prior editions of this book, my long time editor at Springer, Sharon Panulla, and I decided that it was time to update the material to include the most recent trends in evidence-based assessment and interviewing for a wide range of disorders and issues. However, the basic premise that a book of this nature needs to encompass a theoretical rationale, clinical description, and the pragmatics of “how to” once again has been dutifully followed. Thus, the reader will find consistencies between this new 6th edition and the prior ones that have been published. We still believe that students definitely need to read the material covered herein with consummate care. We are particularly concerned that in the clinical education of our graduate students, interviewing unfortunately continues to be given insufficient attention. Considering that good interviewing leads to appropriate clinical and research targets, we continue to underscore the critical importance of this area of training.

Many years have elapsed since publication of the first edition, and many developments in the field have occurred, including repeated revisions of the DSM system of classification and diagnosis. However, the basic structure of this new edition remains similar to those of the prior ones, in that Part I deals with General Issues, Part II with Specific Disorders, and Part III with Special Populations.
